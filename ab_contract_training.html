Module 1 of 9 — The Contract

Parties, Property & Price

Sections 1–5 of the Purchase & Sales Agreement. These are the first fields a seller sees — and the first you must complete correctly.

Customizable Fields — Quick Reference

SectionFieldReqNotes
§1Seller Name(s)YESFull legal first + last. No LLCs alone. LLC-held: individual name + LLC.
§2Property AddressYESStreet, city, county, state — all four required.
§2Items NOT IncludedCONDList anything seller keeps. Blank if none.
§3Sale PriceYESAgreed dollar amount.
§5Closing TimeframeYESDefault: 45 business days. Must match §7 Due Diligence period.

§1 — Parties

Identifies Rebuilt Offers, LLC as Buyer and the Seller(s) by legal name.

⚠ CriticalNever list an LLC name alone as Seller. Must always be a person. For LLC-held properties: individual's name + LLC (e.g., "John Smith, XYZ Holdings LLC"). An incorrect entry here can invalidate the contract.
💬 Talking Point — Seller Name
"This section identifies who we are — Rebuilt Offers, LLC as the buyer — and you as the seller. I'll need your full legal name exactly as it appears on the deed."

§2 — Property

Defines what is included in the sale: the land, all structures, and all attached fixtures currently on the property. Anything the seller wants to keep must be listed as "NOT Included."

💬 Talking Point — What's Included
"This section covers everything physically attached to the property — HVAC, plumbing, built-ins, ceiling fans, garage door openers. Anything currently on the property comes with it unless we specifically write it out as excluded."
💬 Talking Point — Exclusions
"Is there anything you'd like to keep that's currently in or on the property? A chandelier, a refrigerator, something sentimental? We can list those as excluded so there's no confusion at closing."

§3 — Sale Price

💬 Talking Point
"Based on our analysis, we're prepared to offer [AMOUNT]. This is an all-cash offer — no bank, no appraisal contingency, no financing to fall through."

§4 — Non-Financing / All Cash

Confirms a cash transaction. No customization required — standard language.

💬 Talking Point
"Because we're a cash buyer, there's no lender involved. That means faster closing, no appraisal delays, and no risk of financing falling through."

§5 — Closing

Sets the closing timeframe from execution. Standard is 45 business days via National Title Services.

⚠ Critical RuleThe closing timeframe in §5 must ALWAYS match the Due Diligence period in §7. If you change one, change both. Default: 45 business days.
💬 Talking Point
"We target closing within 45 business days from today. That gives both sides time to clear title and handle any outstanding items. National Title Services manages the closing process."
Module 2 of 9 — The Contract

Property Condition & Due Diligence

Sections 6–10. The Due Diligence period is the most operationally critical concept in the entire contract.

§6 — Title Policy

Rebuilt purchases title insurance through National Title Services at Buyer's expense. No seller action needed.

💬 Talking Point
"We purchase title insurance on every deal — it protects against any title issues that come up. Handled entirely by us through National Title Services."

§7 — Property Condition / Due Diligence (AS-IS)

Property purchased AS-IS. The DD period (default: 45 business days, always matching §5) gives Rebuilt the right to access with inspectors, appraisers, and contractors. Rebuilt can terminate in writing before DD expires.

⚠ Never Let DD ExpireIf the DD period expires without a written termination notice, Rebuilt is legally obligated to purchase the property. No exceptions. Create a HubSpot high-priority task 7 days before expiration the moment any contract is executed.
💬 Talking Point — AS-IS
"We're purchasing the property exactly as it sits today — no repairs, no cleaning required. You don't need to fix anything before we close."
💬 Talking Point — Due Diligence
"During our due diligence period, we'll need access to the property for our team — inspectors, contractors, whoever we bring in to evaluate it. This gives us time to fully assess the property before we commit to closing."

§8 — Possession

Seller delivers possession at closing. No exceptions unless documented in §19/§20. Post-occupancy must be negotiated and written into Special Provisions.

💬 Talking Point
"At closing, you hand over the keys and the property transfers to us. If you need a few extra days after closing to move out, we can discuss and document that in Special Provisions."

§9 — Pre-Marketing Agreement

Grants Rebuilt the right to market the property (including MLS) at its discretion. If vacant, seller provides key or lockbox access upon execution.

💬 Talking Point
"Once we're under contract, we have the right to begin marketing the property immediately — listing it online, on the MLS, showing it to potential buyers. If it's vacant, we'll ask for key or lockbox access."

§10 — Prorations

Taxes, insurance, rents, and HOA fees prorated through closing date by National Title. No seller action needed.

💬 Talking Point
"At closing, we divide ongoing costs — property taxes, HOA dues, insurance — based on the exact day we close. You pay your share through closing day; we take over after that."
Module 3 of 9 — The Contract

Default, Expenses & Special Provisions

Sections 11–20. Seller obligations, closing costs, assignment rights, and the Special Provisions field.

§13 — Default

If Seller fails to comply, Rebuilt may enforce specific performance or terminate. Prevailing party in any suit recovers attorney's fees.

💬 Talking Point
"If something prevents you from closing — a title issue you knew about, a change of heart after the DD period — we have the right to either hold you to the contract or walk away. This protects both parties from bad-faith situations."

§14 — Representations

Seller warrants no undisclosed liens or assessments at closing, and existing loans are current. Representations survive closing.

⚠ Watch ForIf a seller discloses judgments or additional liens during the Seller Info Worksheet, flag immediately for your Market Manager and National Title. Do not gloss over this section.

§15 — Sales Expenses

PartyResponsible For
Buyer (Rebuilt)Buyer-specific closing costs as noted in contract.
SellerReleasing existing liens, prepayment penalties, recording fees, transfer taxes, conveyance fees, mortgage payoffs, and all other seller-side obligations.
Seller (if default)Consequential damages including title costs, loan processing, and other closing costs incurred by Rebuilt or its assignee.
💬 Talking Point — Net Proceeds
"You're responsible for clearing any liens, paying off your mortgage, covering transfer taxes, and any other obligations tied to your ownership. This comes out of your sale proceeds at closing — you don't need to bring cash. Whatever you owe comes out first; what's left is your net."

§16 — Resale of Property

💬 Talking Point — Investor Disclosure
"We're transparent: we're investors. We may renovate and resell, rent it out, assign our contract to another buyer, or clear the land for new construction. Any profit from that is ours — that's how we operate."

§17 — Assignment of Contract

💬 Talking Point
"We may transfer our purchase rights to another investor before closing. That investor steps into our shoes — they take on all the same obligations. Your deal terms don't change."

§19/§20 — Special Provisions

⚠ Absolute PolicyUnder NO circumstances may a cancellation clause be added to Special Provisions. Any seller request must be escalated to your Market Manager immediately.
📌 NoteAlways enter "N/A (Not Applicable)" if no special provisions exist. A blank field is never acceptable.

Module 5 of 9 — Supporting Documents

Agency & Authorization Documents

Five documents requiring seller signatures. Each must be explained and executed correctly.

Consumer Guide to Agency / Fair Housing / Affiliated Business Disclosure

Three-part document (one signature page): how Rebuilt Realty operates as a brokerage, Fair Housing compliance, and disclosure of Rebuilt's relationships with National Title Services and Rebuilt Capital, LLC.

💬 Talking Point — Agency
"This explains how Rebuilt Realty operates as a licensed brokerage. In this transaction, we're acting as the buyer — not your agent. We're required to disclose that upfront."
💬 Talking Point — Affiliated Business
"We disclose that we have a relationship with National Title Services and Rebuilt Capital. You're not required to use them, but we're required to tell you about the affiliation."
📌No customizable fields. Seller signature and date required.

Notice of Non-Representation

States that Rebuilt Offers, LLC does not represent the seller. Signing acknowledges receipt only.

💬 Talking Point
"This is our standard disclosure that we represent ourselves as the buyer — not your real estate agent. If you'd like independent representation, you're free to engage your own agent."

Seller's Responsibility Acknowledgement

Module 7 of 9 — Supporting Documents

PA Addendum

Required for Pennsylvania transactions only. Discloses the wholesaling structure to seller and end buyer.

What It Covers

  • Rebuilt is a Wholesaler entering an assignable contract — not a traditional buyer.
  • Rebuilt will sell (assign) its contract interest for a profit. Seller is not entitled to any of that profit.
  • Rebuilt does not represent the seller or end buyer — no fiduciary duty to either party.
  • Agreement is legally binding. Consulting an attorney is recommended.
💬 Talking Point (Pennsylvania Only)
"In Pennsylvania, we're required to disclose how our wholesale process works. We enter into a purchase agreement with you at an agreed price, then may sell our right to purchase to another investor. You get the price we agreed to — any profit above that from our assignment is ours. This form makes sure you understand that clearly."

Module 8 of 9 — Critical Protocols

Due Diligence Protocols

The highest-urgency operational element of any active AB Contract. These protocols must be internalized before working a single live deal.

The Alignment Rule

📐 The RuleDue Diligence Period (§7) = Closing Timeframe (§5). Always. Default: 45 business days for both. If you change one, change the other.

Post-Execution Checklist

The moment a contract is fully executed, complete all of the following immediately:

  • Calculate the DD expiration date (45 business days from execution).
  • Create a HubSpot HIGH PRIORITY task 7 days before the DD expiration date.
  • Label the task: "DD Expiration — [Property Address] — Evaluate: Close / Extend / Retrade / Terminate".
  • Notify Market Manager, Dispositions, and National Title of the DD end date.
⚠ Non-NegotiableIf the DD period expires without action, Rebuilt is legally bound to purchase the property at the contracted price — regardless of condition, title complications, or market changes. This is not recoverable.

DD Scenario Decision Table

Property condition worse than expectedRetrade: request price adjustment or terminateMarket Manager

Seller unresponsive / unreachableEscalate to Market Manager immediatelyMarket Manager NOW

Practice

Role-Play Scenarios

Work through each scenario before revealing the suggested approach. These reflect real situations you will encounter on live calls.

🎭 Scenario 1 — The Net Proceeds Confusion
A seller accepted your offer of $78,000. They have a $45,000 mortgage, $3,200 in back taxes, and a $1,500 HOA lien. The night before closing they ask: "So I'm getting $78,000 tomorrow, right?" How do you respond?
Walk them through the math calmly: "The $78,000 is the purchase price, but your obligations come out of that first. Your mortgage payoff of approximately $45,000, the $3,200 in back taxes, and the $1,500 HOA lien will all be settled at closing from the proceeds. Your net will be approximately $28,300 after those are cleared — you don't need to bring anything to the table. This was outlined in the Seller's Responsibility Acknowledgement you signed. National Title will have the final closing statement for you before closing." Stay calm, don't apologize, and reference the documents they already signed.
🎭 Scenario 2 — The Inherited Property
A caller says: "My mom passed away 8 months ago and left me the house in her will. It's not in probate yet but I want to sell fast — can we sign today?" What do you do?
Do not execute the contract yet. Escalate to your Market Manager and loop in National Title before proceeding. The property is still titled in the deceased mother's name — the caller cannot legally sell it until title is transferred. In Tennessee, if the passing was more than 366 days ago, an Affidavit of Heirship may be an option. Since it has been 8 months, probate may be required. Express empathy, explain that you want to help get this done and that there is a path — but you need to confirm the right process with your title team first. Never promise a quick close on an inherited property until title has reviewed the situation.
🎭 Scenario 3 — The Tenant-Occupied Property
The seller has a tenant paying $1,100/month on a month-to-month lease. The seller also wants to stay in the property themselves for 30 days after closing to "wrap things up." What sections and documents are impacted?
Several things are in play: (1) Seller Info Worksheet — mark Tenant Occupied, capture tenant name, contact, and monthly rent of $1,100 for proration calculations. (2) The seller's request to stay 30 days post-closing is a Post-Occupancy arrangement — it requires a separate Post-Occupancy Agreement with a holdback amount, documented in Special Provisions. (3) Prorations — tenant's rent is prorated through closing; buyer gets credit for any prepaid rent. (4) Photo and Access Authorization — confirm the tenant has been notified and consents to access for photos and inspections. Escalate the post-occupancy request to your Market Manager before finalizing the contract.
🎭 Scenario 4 — The Cancellation Clause Request
A motivated seller is ready to sign but says: "My daughter said I should only sign if there is a 30-day out clause in case I change my mind." How do you handle this?
Do not add any cancellation clause — this is a firm Rebuilt policy with no exceptions. Acknowledge the concern without agreeing: "I completely understand wanting to feel protected. What I can tell you is that our contract already includes a Due Diligence period that gives both sides time to work through any issues — and we're committed to making this as smooth as possible. What I'm not able to do is add a cancellation clause; that's not something we can include in our agreements." If the seller continues to insist, escalate to your Market Manager before signing anything. Never sign a contract with a cancellation clause.
🎭 Scenario 5 — The Pennsylvania Deal
You are closing a deal on a property in Philadelphia, PA. Walk through every additional step and document required compared to a standard state transaction.
In addition to the standard packet (Purchase & Sales Agreement, Seller Info Worksheet, Consumer Guide/Fair Housing/Affiliated Business, Notice of Non-Representation, Authorization to Sign Listing Docs, Authorization for Release of Information, Seller's Responsibility Acknowledgement, and Photo & Access Authorization), Pennsylvania requires the Agency Disclosure Assignable Contract Addendum. Key steps: (1) Confirm the "Wholesaler acting on own behalf" checkbox is selected. (2) Have the seller print their name and sign the Owner acknowledgement block. (3) Rebuilt's signatory — Albert Charles Young, Member + CEO — is pre-populated. (4) The Buyer section is filled in only when the contract is assigned — it may not be completed at seller execution. Explain the wholesaling disclosure clearly using the PA-specific talking point.
